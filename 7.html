Foundational Principles

The Brain as an Open Thermodynamic System

Breaking Boltzmann's Paradigm

Living neural networks exist permanently far from thermal equilibrium. Classical statistical physics rests on detailed balance where forward and backward states match perfectly.

  • Energy Fluxes: The human brain consumes ~20% of metabolic oxygen to preserve ion concentrations against steep electrical barriers.
  • Nonequilibrium Steady State (NESS): Driven by continuous metabolic fuel, sustaining a structural macroscopic Arrow of Time.

Core Theoretical Objective

Our presentation maps out how modern physicists detect, isolate, and numerically solve for this nonequilibrium signature across macroscopic and microscopic fields.

$$\Delta S_{\text{universe}} = \Delta S_{\text{system}} + \Delta S_{\text{surroundings}} > 0$$
Part I – Experimental Methodologies

Measuring Neural Nonequilibrium

The Fundamental Macroscopic Challenge

Neural assemblies execute non-linear interactions hidden at macro scales. Empirical capture depends on proxies (fMRI BOLD, MEG/EEG source fields, multi-unit electrode spiking) inherently masked by structured environmental noise and instrumentation limits.

The absolute objective: isolate, compute, and authenticate an undeniable macroscopic arrow-of-time (entropy production) signature directly from observed multi-variant timeseries.

Experimental Pipeline Structure

Empirical Processing Chain

  • Cortical Parcellation: Segmenting continuous macroarchitecture into structural nodes using standardized atlas coordinate configurations (e.g., Yeo-7, Schaefer-100).
  • Structural Connectome (SC): Resolving physical axonal tracts via Diffusion Tensor Imaging (DTI)—inherently static and undirected.
  • Functional Mapping: Acquiring high-resolution Multivariate Time Series (MVTS) across active regions.
  • Generative Optimization: Tuning parameters so synthetic ensembles reflect empirical functional connectivity.

Surrogate Verification Schemes

Neutralizing Sampling Bias

  • The Bias Phenomenon: Finite structural records generate artificial, non-zero irreversibility metrics even within perfectly symmetric stationary signals.

  • Empirical Separation: Genuine neural paths yield entropy production rates scaling significantly beyond shuffled thresholds.
  • Proof: Verifies the macro-scale arrow of time as a core property of life (Lynn et al. 2021 PNAS).

Part II – Computational Architectures

Modelling & Nonequilibrium Inference

Bridging Data to Statistical Mechanics

Converting passive biophysical observations into exact mathematical formulations requires inverse statistical models. By optimizing continuous stochastic equations and discrete network configurations, we can compute hidden properties and explicitly measure global Entropy Production Rates (EPR).

Helmholtz‑Hodge Vector Field Anatomy

Decomposing Continuous Drift

Any continuous stationary phase-space velocity field $\mathbf{F}(\mathbf{x})$ splits cleanly into symmetric gradients and rotational elements:

$$\begin{aligned} \mathbf{F}_{\text{rev}} &= \mathbf{D}\nabla\log P_{\text{ss}}(\mathbf{x}) \\ \mathbf{F}_{\text{irr}} &= \mathbf{J}_{\text{ss}}(\mathbf{x}) / P_{\text{ss}}(\mathbf{x}) \end{aligned}$$
  • The curl component ($\mathbf{F}_{\text{irr}}$) directly isolates the circulation loops breaking detailed balance.
  • System EPR Equation:
    $$\dot{S}_i = \int \mathbf{F}_{\text{irr}}^{\top} \mathbf{D}^{-1} \mathbf{F}_{\text{irr}} P_{\text{ss}} \, d\mathbf{x} \ge 0$$

Discrete Network Spaces: GHMM Framework

Schnakenberg Entropy Calculations

Gaussian Hidden Markov Models map continuous fMRI pathways into distinct hidden macro states. Transition fluxes yield the exact Schnakenberg EPR formula:

$$\Pi = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{a,b} (q_{ab}P_b - q_{ba}P_a) \log\left(\frac{q_{ab}P_b}{q_{ba}P_a}\right)$$
  • Quantifies the precise directional breaking of detail balance across resting circuits.
  • Active external task engagement shifts transition pathways, scaling overall EPR upward.

Phase Transitions and Criticality

The Edge of Chaos

Whole-brain systems realize peak thermodynamic operational capacity near critical bifurcation boundaries.

  • Hopf Bifurcation Tuning: Adjusting global control parameters ($G$) to balance stable point attractors and sustained oscillations.
  • Information Routing: Operating near the critical point maximizes long-range correlations and optimizes the dynamic workspace.
  • EPR Correlate: Maximizing entropy production corresponds directly to higher cognitive flexibility and active environmental engagement.

Parameter Control Equation

$$\frac{dz_j}{dt} = \left[\rho_j + i\omega_j - |z_j|^2\right]z_j + G \sum_{k} J_{jk} z_k + \eta_j(t)$$
